# Woven Signal

Woven Signal treats every surface as a piece of wayfinding cloth: a system of paths, gates, pauses and bright human markers. The visual language is built from bands, thresholds, stitched lines and dense small details rather than floating decoration. Space is active; it guides movement the way a stewarded festival route guides a crowd. The final expression must feel meticulously crafted, never assembled from generic blocks, with each alignment and interruption carrying the confidence of master-level execution.

Color is ceremonial but controlled. Deep night green and palm black provide the city-after-dark ground; brass, ember, hibiscus and skywash appear as live signals rather than broad gradients. These colors should feel calibrated by hand, with painstaking attention to contrast, rhythm and cultural restraint. The palette borrows the intelligence of textiles and market signage without copying sacred symbols or turning heritage into ornament.

Scale moves between the monumental and the intimate. Large condensed type behaves like a festival poster or a painted gate sign, while small mono labels behave like operations tags, wristbands and route markers. Text stays sparse and purposeful; information lives in spatial hierarchy, status color, route bands and the relationship between modules. The work should look labored over by someone at the top of their field, with quiet evidence of countless hours in the spacing.

Composition is modular but not mechanical. Rectangles, strips and mapped zones repeat like woven warp and weft, then break at deliberate points to create energy. Balance comes from controlled asymmetry: a compressed signal area beside generous breathing room, a bright route stripe cutting through calm fields, a dense map next to a clean instruction. The result should feel human, edited and lived-in, not algorithmically polished.

Motion, when used, is minimal and infrastructural: a route line settling into place, a banner sliding like a physical sign, a status marker pulsing once and stopping. Popups and banners must respect attention, using small, dismissible surfaces tied to genuine visitor needs. SEO and accessibility are part of the craft: semantic content, legible navigation, Event structured data, and mobile-first decisions that do not punish desktop. Every final surface should feel like a polished artifact from a real festival studio, not an AI-generated landing page.

## Design System

### Color Tokens

- Night palm: `#071A15` for primary surfaces and hero atmosphere.
- Forest route: `#0B5D3F` for route confirmations and primary actions.
- Brass light: `#D7A83E` for key CTAs and route highlights.
- Ember clay: `#C6532D` for warnings, stage energy and active marks.
- Hibiscus: `#B82B5E` for cultural accents and sponsor activations.
- Harmattan: `#F5EFE2` for warm surfaces without beige dominance.
- Skywash: `#A8DADC` for cooling service zones and accessibility cues.
- Ink: `#111713` for text.

### Type

- Display: Big Shoulders / condensed, poster-like, used for hero and strong section titles.
- Body: Instrument Sans / humanist, clean, readable on mobile.
- Utility: Geist Mono / operational labels, dates, stand codes, route tags.

### Layout Pattern

The site uses "route bands": horizontal or vertical strips that carry metadata, CTAs or quick actions. Cards stay compact with 8px radii, stronger borders and fewer shadows. Public pages should read like a visitor field guide; portal/admin pages should read like operations surfaces with the same tokens but less ceremony.

### Signature Element

The memorable motif is the Woven Route: a hand-coded grid of bands and dots that suggests Accra streets, kente strip-weaving and festival crowd movement without directly reproducing sacred patterns. It appears in the hero, map treatments, dividers and small status markers.

### Popup / Banner Pattern

Use dismissible announcement bars or bottom slide-ins only. No full-screen interstitials on mobile. Trigger banners by context: schedule updates, gate changes, sponsor activations. Keep copy to one action and one sentence.

### SEO Pattern

Keep public content server-rendered and semantic. Use concise H1s, descriptive section headings, internal links to Programme / Map / Stands, and structured data for the main Event. Avoid hiding navigation on desktop; keep mobile chrome compact and content-first.
